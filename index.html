Part I. - Preliminary

1. Interpretation

Deemed to have come into operation on 29 October 2010. Made under the National Information and Communications Technology Act, 2009.

"Act" means the National Information and Communication Technology Act, 2009 and any regulations made under the Act;
"audiotext hosting service" means an applications service which enables a caller by dialling a short code to receive a recorded message or interact with a programme for the purposes of receiving information;
"bandwidth service" means a network service which enables the transmission of units of information over a link within a particular unit of time;
"broadcasting distribution service" means a network service that distributes television programmes or radio programmes to persons having equipment appropriate for receiving that network service regardless of the means of distribution but does not include:
  • (a) a service (including a teletext service) that provides only data or text; or
  • (b) a service that makes programmes available on demand on a point-to-point basis, including a dial-up service;
"closed user group" shall have the meaning as provided for in Section 2(2).
"community service" means a service provided for community purposes and not operated for profit.
"directory service" means an applications service enabling a person to obtain the telephone number and limited information about a customer which is sought by such person and includes a directory assistance service or published directory service;
"district" means the area defined by reference to Section 72(4) of the Organic Law on Provincial and Local Level Governments;
"earth station" means an apparatus located either on the earth's surface, or within the earth's atmosphere established for communication with one or more space stations or other earth stations by means of satellites or other objects in space;
"international gateway" means any equipment, whether hardware or software, in a network which interfaces that network with an international network;
"international gateway services" means a network service which provides interconnection between a domestic network and an international gateway;
"internet access service" means an applications service whereby a person is able to access Internet services and applications;
"IP telephony" means a packet switched applications service involving the PSTN;
"ITU Convention" means the Constitution and Convention of the International Telecommunication Union signed on 22 December 1992 as amended from time to time.
"Kina Facility Rate" means the rate of that name published by the Bank of Papua New Guinea;
"line link" means a facility that enables communication between two distinct points on a network or networks, other than a radiocommunications link;
"messaging service" means an applications service which involves the storage or forwarding of a message whereby the message is first routed through a central management centre before it is forwarded to the addressee;
"narrowcasting service" means an applications service that supplies content which is intended for a restricted number of viewers or listeners;
"NICTA budget" means the budget prepared under Section 30 of the Act;
"private use" means the use of network services by a closed user group;
"province" has the meaning defined in the Organic Law on Provincial Boundaries;
"PSTN" means a public switched telephone network;
"PSTN telephony" means an applications service involving a PSTN;
"public cellular mobile service" means a network service where an end-user can use a network service while moving continuously between places, the cellular device is not in physical contact with the facility, and the network utilizes intercell hand-over functions;
"public cellular service" means an applications service involving a network of base stations and cells for the delivery of voice and data communications;
"public payphone" means a fixed telephone located in a public place operating via money, token, card, or identification number;
"public payphone service" means an applications service provided by use of a public payphone;
"public switched data service" means an applications service for non-voice services that involves switching of data (telegram, telex, ISDN, ATM);
"radiocommunications link" means a radiocommunications facility that enables communication between two distinct points on a network;
"satellite control station" means an earth station performing telemetry, telecommand, and control of a space station;
"space service" means a network service using a space station;
"space station" means a station located on an object beyond the Earth's atmosphere, including a satellite;
"subscription TV broadcast" means a content applications service made available upon the payment of a subscription fee;
"switching centre / service" means a facility/service that terminates many circuits and routes traffic between them (frame relay, MPLS, IP routing).

Part II. - Operator Licensing

2. Operator Licences

  1. Schedule 1 specifies the types, duration, and conditions of operator licences required to exercise facilities rights or supply ICT services.
  2. NICTA may issue guidelines to clarify the meaning of a "closed user group".
  3. Content services mean broadcasting or narrowcasting services.

3. Network Licence Exemptions

  1. NICTA may exempt a person from holding a network licence by published declaration.
  2. Requires a network licensee to agree in writing to assume all obligations for that person.
  3. NICTA may impose conditions, vary, suspend, or revoke exemptions at any time.

4. Register of Operator Licences

NICTA shall maintain a register of all individual licences, class licence registrations, and exemptions.

5. Lost, etc., Licences

Replacement licences/notices may be issued upon payment of the fee prescribed in Schedule 2.

Part III. - Terms and Conditions

6. Standard Terms for Individual Licences

  • Pay all applicable licence fees and levies.
  • Comply with the Act, laws, radiocommunications licences, and international ITU Conventions.
  • Notify NICTA within 14 days of changes in ownership (>10% per year or >25% total), corporate structures, or joint ventures.
  • Notify NICTA before share/board composition changes exceeding 25%.
  • Take proper safety measures regarding radiation and emissions.
  • Ensure accurate and reliable charging mechanisms.
  • Indemnify NICTA against claims from licensee breaches.

7. Standard Terms for Class Licences

Similar to individual licences: pay fees, comply with laws/ITU rules, ensure safety compliance, verify billing accuracy, and indemnify NICTA.

8. Variation of Individual Licences

Must apply in writing to NICTA detailing proposed variations, reasons, and dates.

9. Saving of Specific Licence Condition

Telikom PNG Limited must continue to maintain and operate the National Relay Service and the National Maritime Radio Safety Service until migration rules apply.

Part IV & V. - Applications & Class Registration

10. Individual Licence Applications

Applications must include detailed business information:

  • Evidence of PNG citizenship (for individuals).
  • Financial plans, capex, opex, and revenue projections.
  • Disaster recovery plans and technical roll-out timelines.
  • Corporate structure: Certificates of Incorporation, constitutions, and latest audited financial accounts.
  • For foreign companies: Investment Promotion Authority (IPA) certificate.
  • CVs, police clearances, and passport copies for directors and major shareholders.

11. Grant of Individual Licences

NICTA evaluates financial standing, technical expertise, business behavior, corporate governance, national security impacts, and spectrum availability. Processing target is 90 days from receipt of full details.

12. Renewal

Must apply at least 60 days before expiration.

13. Procedure for Class Registration

Submitted in duplicate. Requires proof of citizenship or IPA certificate, technical description of services, and the registration fee. Approved applications are endorsed and returned.

Part VI & VII. - Fees and Penalties

14. Licence Fees

Consists of an application fee and an annual fee fixed by the Minister responsible for treasury matters to support NICTA's budget forecasts.

Late Payments: Calculated daily at a rate of 5 percentage points above the Kina Facility Rate per annum, and/or licence suspension.

16. Exemption from Fees

The Minister may exempt or substitute a reduced fee via notice in the National Gazette.

18 & 19. Numbering Fees

NICTA imposes charges for number allocations based on whether the block is defined as a valuable state resource under Section 36 of the Act.

Part VIII. - General Provisions

21. Billing Information

Access providers must, if requested, provide access seekers with detailed billing info (numbers dialled, call times, duration, geographic regions) in an agreed electronic or paper format.

22. Universal Access and Service Levy

Set by NICTA at a level not exceeding 2 percent of net revenues.

23, 24 & 25. ICT Appeals Panel

Parties seeking review must lodge applications to the Secretariat marked for the Chairman of the Panel of Experts. NICTA may request security deposits for costs.
